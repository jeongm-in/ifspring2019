Food

Hawaiian Plate

The plate lunch is a quintessentially Hawaiian meal, roughly analogous to Southern U.S. meat-and-threes. However, the pan-Asian influence on Hawaiian cuisine, and its roots in the Japanese bento, make the plate lunch unique to Hawaii.

Poke

Hawaiian for "to slice" or "cut crosswise into pieces," poke is diced raw fish served either as an appetizer or as a main course and is one of the main dishes of Native Hawaiian cuisine. The traditional Hawaiian poke consists of fish that has been gutted, skinned, and deboned. It is served with traditional condiments.

Acai

Rich in polyphenols, this superfood can protect your cells and make your body stronger. Enjoy this delicious yet healthy snack that comes in bowl, smoothie and juice.

Poi

Poi is primarily the traditional staple food in native cuisine of Hawaii, made from the underground plant stem or corm of the taro plant (known in Hawaiian as kalo).

Laulau

Laulau is a Native Hawaiian cuisine dish. The traditional preparation consisted of pork wrapped in taro or luau leaf. The dish uses taro leaves, salted butterfish, and either pork, beef, or chicken and is usually steamed on the stove.
