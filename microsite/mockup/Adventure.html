Adventure

Snorkeling

Embark on an underwater adventure and observe exotic tropical fish and coral reef. Enjoy the warm Hawaiian water, perfect for all sorts of aquatic activities.

Luau

  • Polynesian Cultural Center
    55-370 Kamehameha Hwy. Laie, Hawaii 96762
    • Luau is a traditional Hawaiian party that is usually accompanied by traditional Hawaiian music and hula. Experience the spirit of Polynesia at Polynesian Cultural Center.

Surfing

  • Waikiki
    • One of the most recognizable beaches in the world, Waikiki was once a playground for Hawaiian royalty. Today, millions of visitors visit these shores every year. With Mount Leahi (Diamond Head) as your backdrop, the calm waters of Waikiki are perfect for a surfing lesson.
  • Waimea Bay
    • Waimea is famous for its 30-foot waves in the winter and is a top place to watch some of the world’s best and bravest surfers. During summer, the water calms considerably and is a great destination for swimming, snorkeling and diving.

Hiking

  • Maili Pillbox Hike Difficulty: ★☆☆
    • The renowned "pillbox," which used to be a watchpost to guard the island, is now a favorite spot to overlook the ocean and surrounding suburbs.
  • Three Peaks / Olomana Trail Difficulty: ★★☆
    • On this trail you will be able to enjoy a great view of Oahu. But mind your steps, the narrow trails and height are unforgiving.
  • Seven Falls Hike Difficulty: ★★★
    • Discover the pristine nature of Hawaii at this secret trail. Venture your way climbing up the waterfalls and rocky stream. Watch out for flash flooding and falls!
